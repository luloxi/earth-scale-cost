How expensive is a chain if everyone used it every day?

Earth-Scale Cost per Transaction asks a simple question: if the whole planet used one chain for a fixed number of daily transactions per person, what would each transaction effectively cost on the actual technology that exists today?

Assumptions

Total people assumed to be active participants in the chain economy.
Includes payments, swaps, app interactions, gaming actions, and social actions.

Interpretation

This is not just raw fee data. It is a capacity-aware affordability view. A chain with ultra-low fees but weak sustainable throughput should rank worse once you force it into global-scale conditions.

SignalMeaning
Low scoreMore plausible for mass daily usage
High scoreEither too expensive, too capacity-constrained, or both

Method

A clean baseline formula you can evolve into a serious public ranking.

Baseline formula

Required TPS = (population × daily transactions per user) ÷ 86,400

Stress Factor = max(1, required TPS ÷ sustainable TPS)

Earth-Scale Cost per Tx = current cost per tx × Stress Factor

You can later replace the stress factor with a better fee elasticity model, DA cost curve, sequencer cost model, or hardware/decentralization penalty.

Why this is useful

Most chain comparisons fail because they compare today’s idle fees, not world-scale demand. This metric forces every network to answer the same question under the same adoption assumption.

That makes it useful for rankings, ecosystem narratives, investor decks, and protocol design debates.
